# NOVA GAMBIT - DETAILED GAME PHASE STRATEGIES

## OPENING PHASE (Turns 1-10)
**Aether Available:** Low (1-10), focus on piece development

### Key Goals
1. **Control center squares (d4, e4, d5, e5)**
   - Position knights toward c3/f3 (white) or c6/f6 (black)
   - Move bishops out on diagonals (c4, f4 for white development)
   - Achieve majority in center = +1 aether/turn bonus from turn 11+

2. **Position pieces near fountains (if available)**
   - Fountains typically occupy center squares
   - Occupying fountain = +2 aether/turn (CRITICAL advantage)
   - Plan: By turn 8-10, have piece adjacent to fountain for take opportunity

3. **Develop knights and bishops FIRST**
   - Move order: Knights first (N-moves most flexible)
   - Then bishops (lock in diagonals)
   - Avoid moving same piece twice (tempo law)
   - Never move King in opening (exposed to tactics)

4. **Avoid early piece trades**
   - Save pieces for power combos (FROST+IMPRISON synergy requires pieces)
   - Equal trades acceptable only for fountain control
   - Minor pieces (N, B) worth 2.5x more in combos than direct value suggests

### Power Usage Strategy
| Power | Cost | Priority | When to Use |
|-------|------|----------|------------|
| **SPAWN** | 6 | NEVER | Too early, spectral expires in 1 turn |
| **FROST** | 8 | LOW (rare) | ONLY if opponent threatens piece worth 300+, very rare opening blunder |
| **FORTIFY** | 14 | NEVER | Way too expensive, save aether |
| **BLINK** | 8 | LOW (rare) | ONLY if piece trapped with no legal moves (rarity) |
| **All Others** | 14+ | NEVER | Far too expensive for opening |

### Aether Spending Policy
- **Turns 1-5:** Hoard only (insufficient aether: 0-4 total)
- **Turn 6-7:** Still hoarding (5-7 aether, Frost almost affordable but no good target)
- **Turn 8-9:** Start evaluating FROST on high-value threats only
- **Turn 10:** Can afford FROST/BLINK; hoard if no compelling target
- **Exception:** If opponent makes HUGE blunder (Queen hanging), cast FROST+follow-up

### Turn-by-Turn Checklist

**Turn 1-2:**
- [ ] Move one pawn to center (e2-e4 or d2-d4 for white; e7-e5 or d7-d5 for black)
- [ ] Generate +1 aether (turn 2 only: now at 1 aether)
- [ ] Current aether: 0 → 1 (insufficient for any power)

**Turn 3-5:**
- [ ] Develop knight (e.g., Nf3 for white)
- [ ] Second pawn advance or bishop development
- [ ] Current aether: 1 → 4 (still insufficient)
- [ ] No power use

**Turn 6-7:**
- [ ] Develop second knight OR first bishop
- [ ] Open diagonals for bishop (move pawn if needed)
- [ ] Current aether: 4 → 6 (FROST almost available)
- [ ] Scout opponent position for FROST targets (none yet)

**Turn 8-10:**
- [ ] Complete basic development (both knights out, both bishops developed or queued)
- [ ] Position piece adjacent to fountain if opportunity exists
- [ ] Current aether: 6 → 10 (FROST / BLINK now affordable)
- [ ] Use FROST ONLY if: opponent piece worth 300+, hanging, no defense
- [ ] Otherwise: continue development, hoard aether

### Positioning for Opening Phase

**White Opening Positions (Example: e4 Opening)**
- Move e-pawn to e4 (control center)
- Move Nf3 (attacks e5 if black plays e5)
- Move Bc4 (targets f7 weakness)
- Move O-O (castle kingside after development)

**Black Defensive Response (vs e4)**
- Move e7-e5 (counter-center control)
- Move Nf6 (develop toward center)
- Move Bc5 or Be7 (flexible positioning)
- Move O-O (castle kingside)

**Fountain Occupation Strategy**
- If fountains appear at d4, e4, d5, e5:
  - Position Rook to d-file by turn 10-12
  - Ready to move onto fountain on turn 11-13
  - Sacrifice pawn to take fountain (+20 aether over 10 turns ROI)

### Anti-Patterns (Do NOT Do)

❌ **Sacrifice material for <3 aether gain** (poor trade-value, material more valuable early)

❌ **Ignore opponent development** (they build threats, you fall behind in piece activity)

❌ **Control edges instead of center** (fountain value minimal on board edges, concentrate pieces centrally)

❌ **Move King early** (exposed to tactics later, castling preferred)

❌ **Cast power before turn 6** (insufficient aether, terrible ROI)

❌ **Use FROST on low-value pieces** (<300 material: pawns, undefended minor pieces in wrong position)

## EARLY MIDGAME PHASE (Turns 11-20)
**Aether Available:** Growing (10-25), time to start using powers

### Key Goals
1. **Establish material advantage**
   - Convert piece activity into captures (your active pieces capture opponent's)
   - Use FROST to weaken opponent defense, then capture
   - IMPRISON key defenders, then take protected pieces
   - Target: +100-300 material advantage by turn 15

2. **Create tactical opportunities**
   - Position Rook adjacent to opponent Queen (setup for IMPRISON)
   - Create passed pawns (isolated pawn on 4th-5th rank)
   - Cluster opponent pieces for DOUBLE_ATTACK
   - Setup: Plan 2-3 power combos in sequence

3. **Prepare for endgame**
   - Maintain piece count (do not accept equal trades unless strategic)
   - Develop passed pawns (these become promotion threats in endgame)
   - Control fountains (lock in +2-4 aether/turn advantage)
   - Aether cap awareness (start thinking about how to spend when reaching 25+)

### Power Combination Strategies

**Combo 1: FROST → CAPTURE**
- Cost: 8 aether
- Setup: Opponent piece worth 300+ attacking your piece or threatening material
- Execution:
  1. Cast FROST on opponent piece (8 aether)
  2. Opponent cannot move that piece for 1 turn
  3. Your next turn: Capture the frozen piece safely
- ROI: 30x (300+ material captured ÷ 8 aether cost)
- Timing: Turn 11+ (when you have 8+ aether consistently)

**Combo 2: FORTIFY → RISKY ATTACK**
- Cost: 14 aether
- Setup: Your piece (Rook/Knight) can attack 2+ opponent pieces, but opponent has counter-pieces
- Execution:
  1. Cast FORTIFY on your attacking piece (14 aether)
  2. Piece gets 1-hit shield (absorbs first capture)
  3. Attack opponent pieces knowing you'll survive 1 recapture
  4. Next turn: Reposition or attack again
- ROI: 15x (enable 300+ material capture with shield safety)
- Timing: Turn 15+ (when you have 14+ aether consistently)

**Combo 3: IMPRISON → CAPTURE (Multi-Turn)**
- Cost: 14 aether (first turn) + 0 (capture later)
- Setup: Opponent piece (Queen/Rook) is adjacent to one of your pieces
- Execution:
  1. Turn N: Cast IMPRISON with your adjacent piece (14 aether)
  2. Opponent Queen/Rook imprisoned, cannot move
  3. Turn N+1+: Now undefended piece behind the imprisoned one? Capture it
  4. OR: Opponent frees prisoner, but lost tempo + your piece gained initiative
- ROI: 41x (Queen removal 900 ÷ 22 aether if also use FROST)
- Timing: Turn 12+ (when you have 14+ aether)

**Combo 4: IMPRISON (Defender) → CAPTURE (Protected Piece)**
- Cost: 14 aether
- Setup: Opponent Bishop defends Opponent Rook (Rook on e5, Bishop on d4 protecting it)
- Execution:
  1. Your Knight adjacent to Bishop
  2. Cast IMPRISON on Bishop (14 aether)
  3. Bishop imprisoned, Rook now undefended
  4. Your Rook captures opponent Rook (500 material)
- ROI: 36x (500 material ÷ 14 aether)
- Timing: Turn 12-18 (core early midgame)

**Combo 5: DOUBLE_ATTACK (Clustered Opponents)**
- Cost: 14 aether
- Setup: 2+ undefended opponent pieces within reach of your Rook/Queen
- Execution:
  1. Cast DOUBLE_ATTACK with your Rook (14 aether)
  2. First action: Capture Knight on d5 (320 material)
  3. Second action: Capture Bishop on d4 (320 material) from same Rook
  4. Total: 640 material captured in 1 turn
- ROI: 46x (640 material ÷ 14 aether)
- Timing: Turn 14+ (need multiple undefended pieces)

### Aether Spending Policy by Level

| Aether Level | Spending Strategy |
|---|---|
| **10-13** | Only move, no powers (insufficient for impactful powers except Frost) |
| **14-16** | Consider FROST or IMPRISON on high-value targets only |
| **17-20** | BALANCED mode: Use powers when ROI >= 30x |
| **21-25** | AGGRESSIVE: All powers now viable, consider mid-tier frequently |
| **26-30** | DUMP mode: Must spend to avoid cap (hit cap = -3 aether/turn wastage) |

### Fountain Control Strategy

**Fountain Value Calculation:**
- Each fountain: +2 aether/turn
- 1 fountain occupied: +2/turn = +20 aether over 10 turns = 1 IMPRISON power
- 2 fountains occupied: +4/turn = +40 aether over 10 turns = 2 IMPRISON powers or 1 VENGEANCE power

**Fountain Occupation Tactics:**
1. By turn 12-13: Move piece onto fountain square (e.g., Rook to d4)
2. Maintain occupation for rest of game (defend with other pieces if attacked)
3. If opponent occupies fountain: Do not trade piece for fountain (unless major advantage)
4. Sacrifice strategy: Pawn sacrifice to take fountain = justified if game lasts 10+ more turns

**Opponent Fountain Prevention:**
- If opponent piece on fountain, do not let them hold it
- Counter: Move your piece to same fountain OR trade pieces ON fountain
- Net: You lose piece, opponent loses fountain control (acceptable trade)

### Turn-by-Turn Checklist (Turns 11-20)

**Turn 11-13 (Early Mid):**
- [ ] Evaluate aether level (should be 12-18 by turn 13)
- [ ] Identify FROST targets (opponent pieces worth 300+)
- [ ] Scout fountain positions; plan move onto fountain
- [ ] Create passed pawn (advance isolated pawn toward 5th rank)
- [ ] Power use: FROST if good target exists, otherwise hoard

**Turn 14-16 (Early-Mid Transition):**
- [ ] Aether now 14-22 (IMPRISON now affordable)
- [ ] Position piece adjacent to opponent Queen/Rook (setup IMPRISON)
- [ ] If opponent has passed pawn: Freeze it with FROST
- [ ] Fountain control locked in (you occupy 1-2, or forced into counter)
- [ ] Power use: IMPRISON on key defender, or DOUBLE_ATTACK on clusters

**Turn 17-20 (Late Early-Mid):**
- [ ] Aether 20-28 (cap approaching)
- [ ] If aether > 25: Consider PROMOTE on pawn 5th rank (if safe)
- [ ] Opponent threat assessment: Do they have 18+ aether? (Vengeance threat)
- [ ] Continue passed pawn push (advance to 6th rank if unchallenged)
- [ ] Power use: Mix of IMPRISON, DOUBLE_ATTACK, PROMOTE, or dump powers if near cap

### Anti-Patterns (Do NOT Do)

❌ **FROST random pieces** (ROI < 200 material = waste of 8 aether)

❌ **Ignore fountain control** (leaves +4/turn advantage to opponent over 10 turns = 40 aether = 2 big powers)

❌ **Use CHRONOBREAK on minor losses** (<200 material value, 20 aether cost too high)

❌ **PROMOTE without checking opponent Vengeance** (if opponent has 18+ aether, new Queen dies to Vengeance immediately)

❌ **DOUBLE_ATTACK on defended pieces** (first capture gets recaptured, net loss of material)

❌ **Wall in sparse midgame** (18 aether cost wasteful when pieces still active, better used on VENGEANCE)

❌ **Let aether cap wastefully** (every turn capped at 30 = -3 aether lost generation = -300 aether over 100 turns)

## LATE MIDGAME PHASE (Turns 21-30)
**Aether Available:** High (15-30), now spend aggressively

### Key Goals
1. **Convert aether advantage into material advantage**
   - Use VENGEANCE to destroy opponent Queen/Rook
   - Use PROMOTE to advance passed pawns (900 material per promotion)
   - Use DOUBLE_ATTACK + IMPRISON combos to remove multiple key pieces
   - Target: Material advantage +300-500 by turn 25

2. **Weaken opponent's defense**
   - FROST their best defender (Queen/Rook)
   - IMPRISON King escape squares with WALL
   - Use AETHER_BLOCK to silence opponent power responses
   - Goal: Opponent reduced to defensive moves only

3. **Prevent opponent's power spike**
   - If opponent 20+ aether: Cast AETHER_BLOCK (prevents them spending)
   - Alternative: Use VENGEANCE on their Queen before they can power
   - Alternative: Use CHRONOBREAK if they just captured major piece
   - Principle: Disrupt opponent's threatening setup

### Power Usage Strategy by Aether Level

| Aether | Power Recommendations | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **15-19** | FROST, IMPRISON, DOUBLE_ATTACK | Frost opponent Queen (8), then capture later |
| **20-24** | PROMOTE, VENGEANCE (conservative) | Promote pawn 6th rank to Queen (15) |
| **25-29** | VENGEANCE, WALL, AETHER_BLOCK (aggressive) | Destroy opponent Queen (18) |
| **30 (CAPPED)** | MUST SPEND: Any power costs seem cheap now | Use CHRONOBREAK to dump 20, or VENGEANCE (18) to dump |

### Power Combination Sequences

**Sequence 1: FROST → IMPRISON → DOUBLE_ATTACK (3-Power Combo, Cost: 36 aether)**
- Turn N: Opponent Queen on d4, Bishop on c3, Knight on e5
- Your pieces: Rook on d5 (adjacent to Queen), Knight on c4 (adjacent to Bishop)
- Turn N-0.5:
  1. Cast FROST on Queen (8) → Queen frozen
  2. Cast IMPRISON on Bishop with your Knight (14) → Bishop imprisoned
  3. Cast DOUBLE_ATTACK with your Rook (14) → Rook captures Knight (e5), then moves to recapture Rook defender
- Result: Queen frozen, Bishop trapped, 2 pieces captured = material swing +600

**Sequence 2: FORTIFY + DOUBLE_ATTACK (Cost: 28 aether)**
- Setup: Your Rook on e4, opponent Rook on e3 (attacking), opponent Knight on d4 (also threatening)
- Your Rook can reach both pieces in 2 moves
- Execution:
  1. Cast FORTIFY on Rook (14) → Rook shielded (absorbs 1 capture)
  2. Opponent attacks, shield absorbs hit (Rook survives)
  3. Your turn: DOUBLE_ATTACK with Rook (14) → Captures Knight (d4) then Rook (e3)
- Result: Survive attack, counter with 2 captures = material swing +820

**Sequence 3: AETHER_BLOCK + VENGEANCE (Cost: 34 aether)**
- Setup: Opponent has 20 aether, Queen hanging on d4 (your setup: you just moved Rook away)
- Turn N:
  1. Cast AETHER_BLOCK (16) → Opponent cannot spend aether next turn
  2. Your move ends
- Turn N+1 (Opponent): Cannot cast Vengeance (aether blocked), must make chess move, King now in your threat zone
- Turn N+1 (Your turn):
  1. Cast VENGEANCE on Queen (18) → Queen destroyed
  2. Opponent has no counter (aether block still active)
- Result: Queen (900) removed safely

**Sequence 4: PROMOTE + WALL (Cost: 33 aether)**
- Setup: Your pawn on d6 (6th rank), opponent Rook on d5 preventing 7th rank push
- Turn N:
  1. Cast PROMOTE on d-pawn (15) → d-pawn becomes Queen
  2. New Queen on d6 (safe from opponent Rook capture due to distance/defense)
- Turn N+1:
  1. Cast WALL around new Queen (18) → 3 pawns spawn protecting new Queen
  2. Fortress formed: Queen protected by 3 pawns + your pieces
- Result: Unbreakable fortress, new Queen drives endgame

### Passed Pawn Promotion Strategy

**When Pawn Reaches 6th Rank:**
- Check: Opponent aether < 18 (cannot Vengeance your new Queen)
- Check: Your aether >= 15 (can afford PROMOTE)
- Check: Pawn defended or new Queen will have escape squares
- Execution: Cast PROMOTE → Pawn becomes Queen → New Queen on 6th rank (1 square from promotion)

**When Pawn Reaches 7th Rank (EMERGENCY):**
- Opponent MUST deal with pawn immediately (or loses to promotion)
- Opponent options: FROST pawn, IMPRISON pawn, capture pawn
- Your defense: FORTIFY pawn (shield absorbs capture), then PROMOTE on opponent's skip turn
- Alternative: PROMOTE immediately (pawn becomes Queen, game usually over)

**Material Value of Promotion:**
- Pawn → Queen = +800 material (8 Pawns' value in 1 piece)
- Cost: 15 aether
- ROI: 53x (800 ÷ 15)
- Timing: Turn 22+ (should have 15+ aether by now)

### Opponent Threat Assessment

**If Opponent Has 18+ Aether:**
- Threat: They can cast VENGEANCE on your Queen (18 cost)
- Defense option 1: FORTIFY your Queen (14) → Shield absorbs Vengeance, Queen survives
- Defense option 2: Reposition your Queen (move away) → No capture, no Vengeance
- Defense option 3: AETHER_BLOCK their aether (16) → Prevent Vengeance
- Recommendation: Move Queen away (no power cost) or prepare FORTIFY

**If Opponent Has 25+ Aether:**
- Threat: They can afford multiple expensive powers (VENGEANCE + WALL + PROMOTE)
- Your counter: Cast AETHER_BLOCK (16) + VENGEANCE (18) = 34 aether to disable + destroy
- Alternative: Race them (your promoted Queen vs their powers)

**If Opponent Has 30 Aether (CAPPED):**
- Threat: Minimal short-term (capped = no aether generation until they spend)
- Opportunity: They MUST spend, probably on weak power
- Your play: Attack aggressively (they cannot respond with aether-based defense)

### Turn-by-Turn Checklist (Turns 21-30)

**Turn 21-23 (Late Mid Transition):**
- [ ] Aether level: 20-28 (getting close to cap)
- [ ] Passed pawn status: Any pawns on 5th-6th rank?
- [ ] Opponent threat: Do they have 18+ aether? (Vengeance threat)
- [ ] Power plan: PROMOTE if pawn ready, or VENGEANCE if Queen vulnerable
- [ ] Decision: Spend or hoard? (If >25 aether, lean toward spending)

**Turn 24-26 (Late Mid Peak):**
- [ ] Aether likely 24-30 (cap zone)
- [ ] CAP MANAGEMENT: If 28+, must spend within 2 turns (to avoid wastage)
- [ ] Promotion threats: Any opponent pawns on 5th+? Freeze them
- [ ] Endgame prep: Start positioning for pawn races (passed pawns toward 7th rank)
- [ ] Power use: PROMOTE, VENGEANCE, WALL, or CHRONOBREAK (no weak powers)

**Turn 27-30 (Late Mid Endgame Transition):**
- [ ] Aether management: If capped (30), FORCE spending every turn
- [ ] Piece exchange: Trade efficiently for opponent's Queen/Rook
- [ ] Pawn races: Create additional passed pawns if ahead
- [ ] Prepare for endgame: Ensure material advantage (2+ pieces) going in
- [ ] Power budget: Plan Chronobreak/Vengeance uses for endgame emergencies

### Anti-Patterns (Do NOT Do)

❌ **Ignore opponent's 18+ aether** (they will Vengeance your Queen, leaving you defenseless)

❌ **Use CHRONOBREAK on minor material** (<400 material: costs 20 aether, ROI < 20x)

❌ **Waste power on King** (King cannot be targeted by VENGEANCE, DOUBLE_ATTACK, or IMPRISON by rule)

❌ **Promote without defense** (new Queen surrounded by 2+ enemy pieces = immediate recapture)

❌ **WALL in wrong direction** (Wall should protect King or passed pawn, not random squares)

❌ **Spend all aether early then cap out** (prevent capping by strategic spending at 25, not 30)

❌ **IMPRISON piece that's already defending nothing** (low ROI < 100)

## ENDGAME PHASE (Turns 31+)
**Aether Available:** Variable (depends on pressure), use very carefully

### Key Goals
1. **Enforce pawn promotion threats**
   - Promote any pawn 6th-7th rank to Queen immediately (15 aether cost)
   - Use WALL to protect passed pawns from capture
   - Block opponent's passed pawns with FROST/IMPRISON
   - Goal: Achieve 2+ Queens before opponent

2. **Deliver checkmate**
   - Use WALL to restrict opponent King escape squares (3 pawns = fortress)
   - Use BLINK to approach King strategically (each square closer = +30 material value)
   - Use IMPRISON on last defender near opponent King
   - Goal: Opponent King trapped, mate in 2-3 moves

3. **Prevent endgame mating attacks**
   - Use CHRONOBREAK to undo opponent's mating sequence (20 aether, expensive but saves game)
   - Use FORTIFY on defending pieces (Rook/Queen near King)
   - Use BLINK to escape King from mating net
   - Goal: Survive until your passed pawn promotes or mates them

### Aether Economy in Endgame

**Generation Rate:** +3 aether/turn (baseline) + up to +4 from fountains = +7/turn maximum

**Capping Behavior:**
- With 2 fountains: Hit 30 aether cap every 4-5 turns
- Without fountains: Hit 30 aether cap every 10 turns
- Capped aether = worthless (0 generation) = EMERGENCY SPEND

**Spending Priorities at Cap (30/30):**
1. VENGEANCE on opponent Queen/Rook/passed pawn (18 aether)
2. CHRONOBREAK if opponent just captured major piece (20 aether)
3. PROMOTE on your passed pawn (15 aether)
4. WALL to create fortress or protect pawn (18 aether)
5. ANY power if desperately capped (even weak powers beat 0 generation)

|---|---|---|---|
| **VENGEANCE** | 35% | Opponent Queen/Rook/passed pawn on 6-7 | 18 |
| **CHRONOBREAK** | 30% | Opponent mating attack OR captured Rook/Queen | 20 |
| **WALL** | 25% | Create fortress OR protect passed pawn | 18 |
| **BLINK** | 25% | King approach OR escape mating net | 8 |
| **CLEANSE** | 15% | Free trapped high-value piece | 14 |

| **FORTIFY** | 12% | Shield King or defending Rook | 14 |
| **AETHER_BLOCK** | 8% | Opponent 20+ aether (Chronobreak threat) | 16 |

| **SPAWN** | 2% | Almost never (spectral expires in 1 turn) | 6 |

### Passed Pawn Emergency Rules

**PAWN ON 5TH RANK:**
- Action: Start PROMOTE countdown (next 2 turns to 7th rank)
- Opponent response: FROST pawn (freeze it), advance other piece
- Your defense: CLEANSE to unfreeze, then PROMOTE

**PAWN ON 6TH RANK:**
- Action: EMERGENCY - high promotion threat (becomes 7th rank next move)
- Opponent response: Capture pawn OR FROST pawn
- Your defense: FORTIFY pawn (shield absorbs capture) → Next turn PROMOTE

**PAWN ON 7TH RANK:**
- Action: CRISIS - pawn promotes to Queen on opponent's skip turn OR your next turn
- Opponent response: MUST capture pawn (only move that matters)
- Your defense: If opponent captures → You're down 100 material but game was already endgame. If opponent doesn't capture → You promote next turn (game over)
- Outcome: Promote at cost of pawn capture is acceptable (Queen gained worth 900, pawn lost worth 100 = net +800)

### Mating Net Construction

**Step 1: Restrict King Escape (Turn 31-35)**
- Identify opponent King position (e.g., King on g1)
- Scan escape squares: f1, f2, g2, h2, h1 (up to 5 squares)
- WALL around King: Cast WALL (18) to spawn 3 pawns on f1, f2, g2 (assuming empty)
- Result: King escape squares reduced from 5 to 2 (h1, h2)

**Step 2: Position Attacking Pieces (Turn 35-38)**
- Move Rook to 7th rank (threatening King zone)
- Move other Rook/Queen to 6th rank (escape square control)
- BLINK pieces closer if blocked (8 aether per BLINK)
- Result: 2-3 pieces surrounding King in small area

**Step 3: Deliver Checkmate (Turn 38-40)**
- Opponent King now has 0-1 escape squares (caged by WALL pawns)
- Your Rook move: Rook checks King from 1st rank
- Opponent King cannot move (all squares attacked or blocked)
- Checkmate achieved

### Endgame Piece Valuation

**In endgame, pieces are worth 3-5x more than middlegame valuation:**

| Piece | Mid-game Value | Endgame Value | Reason |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Queen** | 900 | 2700+ | No defending pieces, Queen dominates board |
| **Rook** | 500 | 1500+ | Rook on 7th rank = near-mate threat |
| **Bishop** | 330 | 700+ | Long diagonals = fewer pieces to block |
| **Knight** | 320 | 400 | Knights weaker in endgame (fewer pieces to jump over) |
| **Pawn** | 100 | 900 | Passed pawn on 6-7 = promotion threat |

**King Distance Value:**
- Each square closer to enemy King = +30 material points
- Example: Your King 5 squares from opponent King → Move 1 square closer → +30 value (now 4 squares away)

- [ ] Pawn race: Advance passed pawns toward 7th rank
- [ ] King confinement: Start WALL if opponent King has 3+ escape squares
- [ ] Piece activity: Position Rook for 7th rank penetration

**Turn 37-40 (Endgame Peak):**
- [ ] Promotion phase: Any pawns on 6th+? PROMOTE on next aether availability
- [ ] Mating setup: King now has 1-2 escape squares? Prepare Rook checks
- [ ] Opponent Chronobreak readiness: Do they have 20+ aether? (Prepare for undo)
- [ ] Survival: If down material, use CHRONOBREAK liberally to reset

**Turn 41+ (Drawn Out Endgame):**
- [ ] Patience: Methodically advance passed pawns
- [ ] Cap management: Spend aether aggressively (rotation of FROST/PROMOTE/VENGEANCE)
- [ ] Decision point: If 2+ pawns advanced, should have won
- [ ] Resignation: If down 2+ pieces with no passed pawn, consider resignation

### Anti-Patterns (Do NOT Do)

❌ **Ignore opponent's passed pawn on 6th rank** (will become 7th rank next turn, then promotion)

❌ **Use FROST on unimportant pieces** (endgame ROI must be 30x+, only use on promotion threats)

❌ **Let aether cap waste generation** (every turn capped at 30 = -3 aether lost, -300 over 100 turns)

❌ **Use CHRONOBREAK for <300 material swing** (too expensive 20 aether, only for game-saving undoes)

❌ **PROMOTE without checking King safety** (new Queen surrounded by 2+ enemies = immediate recapture, dead Queen)

❌ **WALL on wrong side of King** (should restrict escape toward opponent piece, not random pawns)

❌ **Trade pieces equal when ahead** (up material, trade pieces → pawn endgame → high variance)

## DANGEROUS POSITIONS - USE POWERS DEFENSIVELY

### Situation 1: King in Check
**Priority:** Resolve immediately (check is forcing)

**Options (in priority order):**
1. **Move King** (no aether cost) → Best if escape square available
2. **Block check with piece** (no aether cost) → Only if Rook check and piece can block
3. **Capture checking piece** (no aether cost) → Only if piece undefended
4. **VENGEANCE checking piece** (18 aether) → Last resort, expensive but wins game if Queen checking
5. **CHRONOBREAK opponent's move** (20 aether) → Ultra-last resort if mated in 1 with check

**Example:** Opponent Rook checks your King on e1 from e8
- Option 1: Move King to d1 or f1 (check resolved, no cost)
- Option 2: Block with piece (e.g., Rook to e4 blocks along e-file)
- Option 3: If your Queen on e5 adjacent, capture Rook on e8 with Queen (no aether cost)
- Option 4: VENGEANCE to destroy Rook (18 aether, excessive)

### Situation 2: Material Down (Disadvantage -300+)
**Priority:** Regain material through power combos

**Strategy:**
1. **IMPRISON defender** (14) → Frees up protected piece for capture
2. **FROST opponent Queen** (8) → Disable their strongest piece
3. **DOUBLE_ATTACK** (14) → Capture 2 undefended pieces
4. **CHRONOBREAK if they just captured major piece** (20) → Undo and get piece back

**Example:** You down 1 Rook (500 material). Opponent has Queen (d4) defending Rook (e5).
- Turn N: IMPRISON Queen with your adjacent piece (14 aether)
- Turn N+1: CAPTURE now-undefended Rook (500 material)
- Result: Down 1 Rook → Now captured 1 Rook = material back to even

### Situation 3: Facing Mate Threat
**Priority:** Block or escape mate immediately

**Defense powers:**
1. **WALL around King** (18) → Spawn 3 pawns, create escape squares
2. **BLINK King away** (8) → Teleport King to safety zone
3. **CHRONOBREAK opponent move** (20) → Undo their mating setup
4. **FORTIFY King** (14) → Shield absorbs 1 capture (usually not enough for mate threat)

**Example:** Opponent Queen on e7 + Rook on e1 threatening mate on e1. King on d1 (no escape).
- Option 1: WALL around King (18) → Spawn pawns on c1, d2, e2 → King now has escape to c2
- Option 2: BLINK King away (8) → King teleports to d6 (away from attack)
- Option 3: CHRONOBREAK (20) → Undo entire opponent turn, pieces reposition

### Situation 4: Opponent at 25+ Aether (Power Spike Threat)
**Priority:** Disrupt their power sequence before they cast

**Defensive powers:**
1. **AETHER_BLOCK opponent** (16) → They gain 0 aether next turn, cannot power
2. **VENGEANCE on their Queen** (18) → Remove strongest piece before they power
3. **FROST their best piece** (8) → Disable before they can combo
4. **BLINK your pieces away** (8) → Reposition before they attack

**Example:** Opponent has 26 aether, Queen on d4 (undefended after you moved Rook). They threaten to PROMOTE passed pawn + VENGEANCE your Queen = game over.
- Option 1: AETHER_BLOCK (16) → They gain 0 aether, must make chess move
- Option 2: VENGEANCE their Queen (18) → Remove their strongest attacker
- Combined: If you do both (34 aether total) → Queen gone + they cannot power → You survive

### Situation 5: Opponent Pawn on 7th Rank (Promotion Threat)
**Priority:** Stop pawn immediately (becomes Queen next turn)

**Defense powers (in order of efficiency):**
1. **FROST pawn** (8) → Pawn frozen, cannot promote for 1 turn (your next turn: capture or PROMOTE your pawn first)
2. **VENGEANCE pawn** (18) → Destroy pawn entirely (expensive but permanent solution)
3. **IMPRISON pawn** (14) → Pawn caged, cannot move for multiple turns
4. **Capture pawn with piece** (0 aether) → Best if possible (costs nothing)

**Example:** Opponent pawn on e7, 1 square from promoting to Queen. Your King on e1, cannot reach in 1 move.
- Option 1: FROST pawn (8) → Pawn frozen, next turn you capture with King (e1-e7 will take 6 moves, but pawn frozen so no promotion)
- Option 2: VENGEANCE pawn (18) → Pawn destroyed, gone forever (expensive but clean)
- Option 3: IMPRISON pawn with your adjacent piece (14) → Pawn caged, promotion blocked

## CRITICAL HEURISTICS FOR DECISION-MAKING

### Heuristic 1: Fountain = +1 Aether/Turn Territory Control
- **Rule:** Each occupied fountain = +2 aether/turn
- **10-turn window:** 2 fountains = +40 aether cumulative = 2-3 big powers
- **Decision:** Sacrifice Pawn (100 material) to take fountain if game will last 10+ more turns
- **Example:** Turn 12, opponent has fountain on d4, your Pawn can take it → Sacrifice Pawn, move to d4 → Break even in 2 turns, profit afterward

### Heuristic 2: NEVER Trade Equal Material in Early Midgame
- **Rule:** Hold pieces for power combos (FROST+IMPRISON = 2 pieces -> 1 Queen = 900 material)
- **Midgame piece value multiplier:** 2.5x (pieces worth more in combos than direct value)
- **Exception:** Trade if fountain control or prevents passed pawn
- **Example:** Your Knight attacked by Pawn → DON'T trade Knight for Pawn (2x more valuable in combo). BLINK Knight away (8 aether) instead

- **Combo cost:** 8 + 18 = 26 aether
- **Effect:** Turn N: FROST Queen (8) → Disable Queen for 1 turn. Turn N+1: VENGEANCE on Queen (18) → Destroy Queen. Total 2-turn combo = Queen dead + opponent lost 1 turn
- **ROI:** 35x (900 material ÷ 26 aether)
- **When:** Mid-late game when opponent Queen is active threat

### Heuristic 4: At 30/30 Aether - MUST SPEND Every Turn
- **Rule:** Sitting at 30 aether = 0 generation = -3 aether/turn opportunity cost
- **Math:** 10 turns capped = -30 aether wasted = cost of 2 VENGEANCE powers
- **Decision:** Even weak power (SPAWN 6) beats capped hoarding
- **Rotation:** Spend 15-20 aether each turn → Next turn gains 3 → Spend again → Loop

### Heuristic 5: CHRONOBREAK Only When Absolutely Necessary (20 Aether Cost)
- **Rule:** 20 aether buys 1 VENGEANCE or 1 WALL or PROMOTE + small power
- **Decision threshold:** Material swing >300 (ROI must be >15x)
- **Example usage:** Opponent just captured your Queen (900) → CHRONOBREAK justified (ROI 45x)
- **Example waste:** Opponent just captured your Pawn (100) → CHRONOBREAK wasteful (ROI 5x)

### Heuristic 6: Passed Pawn on 7th Rank = Emergency Override Everything
- **Rule:** Passed pawn on 7th rank becomes Queen next turn (900 material gain)
- **Priority:** Highest (even above defense in some cases)
- **Action:** PROMOTE if aether available (15 cost for 900 Queen = 60x ROI)
- **Emergency spend:** If no aether, burn all other pieces to get 15 aether for PROMOTE

### Heuristic 7: Opponent Queen Hanging = Highest Priority Target
- **Rule:** Undefended Queen (900 material) = free 900 if reachable
- **Decision:** VENGEANCE (18 aether) if cannot capture directly with piece
- **ROI:** 50x (900 ÷ 18)
- **When:** Opponent makes positional mistake, Queen left undefended after capture sequence

### Heuristic 8: Piece Adjacency = Setup for IMPRISON
- **Rule:** If your piece adjacent to opponent Queen/Rook, IMPRISON becomes available (14 aether)
- **Value:** Queen (900) caged for 1+ turns = tempo win + threat elimination
- **Positioning:** Actively seek positions where your Rook/Queen can reach opponent Queen/Rook
- **Example:** Your Rook on e5, opponent Queen on d5 (1 square) → IMPRISON Queen (14) → Queen stuck for 1+ turns

- **Rule:** 2+ undefended opponent pieces within reach of your piece = DOUBLE_ATTACK (14 aether)
- **Efficiency:** Each piece captured = 320-500 material
- **Example:** Your Rook can reach Knight (d5) and Bishop (d4) in sequence → DOUBLE_ATTACK (14) → 640 material
- **ROI:** 46x

### Heuristic 10: Fortress Formation = King Protection
- **Rule:** WALL around King + adjacent pawns = unbreakable fortress (3+ pawns around King)
- **Cost:** WALL = 18 aether
- **Durability:** Fortress holds against all attacks (opponent needs 3+ pieces to break)
- **When:** Late endgame when opponent has Queen + Rook (mate threats)
- **Example:** King on e2, Wall on d1-e1-f1 + your pawns → King trapped but safe → Survive indefinitely

### Transition: Opening → Early Midgame (Turn 10 → Turn 11)

**Before Turn 11:**
- [ ] Aether level: 9-10 (can afford FROST if target exists)
- [ ] Material: Roughly equal (no major advantages)
- [ ] Piece development: Both knights out, both bishops developed or on queues
- [ ] Passed pawns: None yet (still opening structure)
- [ ] Fountain control: Pieces positioned near fountains, not yet occupied

**At Turn 11+:**
- [ ] Aether generation rate increases: Now +2/turn (was +1/turn)
- [ ] Power spending begins: FROST and IMPRISON now high priority
- [ ] Fountain occupation: Move piece onto fountain square
- [ ] Passed pawn creation: Start isolating pawn toward 5th rank
- [ ] Material swing: First captures should occur (FROST+CAPTURE combos)

**Checklist Items:**

- [ ] Material: Should have +100-200 advantage if playing well
- [ ] Passed pawn status: At 5th rank (ready for 6th rank push)

- [ ] Cap management begins: Start worrying about hitting 30 (will hit by turn 25)

- [ ] Passed pawns: Should have 1-2 on 5th-6th rank ready for push

**Checklist Items:**
